Description

Pixel Man's Calorie Calculator is a redesigned of Pixel Calorie Calculator. The new version is a JavaScript port of the original Adobe Flash ActionScript.

Ideation

The original Pixel Calorie Calculator was developed for an assignment for my interaction design module which I took in my third year in the National University of Singapore. The assignment called for us to create an utility application in ActionScript. The advice given by the teaching assistants was to develop a simple calculator that can perform basic arithmetic calculations.

Back then, I just started working out in the gym regularly, and realised that I should create something different instead of following the rest of the class and build yet another arithmetic calculator. I thus designed an application which helps users to calculate their body mass index and their daily required calories. I am also interested in pixel art design. Hence, I decided to marry these two elements together, and the Pixel Calorie Calculator was born. Fast forward a year later, I decide to redesign and port the application to JavaScript as the support for ActionScript applications have dwindle over the years.

Brand identity

I decide to keep the original design of brand logo to retain its pixel art look. To humanise the brand, I named the stick man in the brand logo as Pixel Man. I believe naming the character instead of referring it simply as pixel stick man gives a stronger identification to the brand.

Specifications

Layout

A single column design was used for the layout to allow the content to flow from top to bottom. The label of each form field is positioned on the left of the field as it follows the natural reading flow of the users' eyes which make the content easier to understand by the users. I have also included a help text after each form field to instruct the users on the range of accepted values. The help test also acts as a status label for the form fields, and will provide real time alert to the users if the values which they have entered are invalid. The results section of the web application is hidden until the Calculate button is clicked by the users. This follows the responsive disclosure design pattern which aims to hide the additional complexity from the users, and allow them to focus on the primary task, which is to enter the correct values in the form fields.

Icons

Three of the icons designed for this project are used for displaying the status of the users' inputs. Two of them were used in the personal contact found in the footer section.
